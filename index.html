0340 ZULU. Iranian anti-ship ballistic missiles have struck the destroyer USS Milius in the Strait of Hormuz. Seven sailors are dead. Tehran claims the strike was retaliation for a covert action it attributes to the United States.

The Joint Chiefs are assembled. CENTCOM is requesting strike authority. This is no longer a crisis to be managed — it is a war to be won.

Win it. Destroy Iran's nuclear program and break its ability to fight — its missile force, its navy, its command structure — before the casualty count, the home front, or the global economy breaks you first. Tehran may eventually sue for peace, but only from its knees: no one is coming to save you at the table.

This is a work of strategic fiction, in the tradition of DEFCON and Twilight Struggle. The scenario, events, and outcomes are invented and abstracted for gameplay. It depicts no real events and endorses no policy.
